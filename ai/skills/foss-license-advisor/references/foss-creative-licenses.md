# Free Licenses for Creative Works

## Creative Commons Free Licenses

**Important:** Only some Creative Commons licenses are considered "free culture" licenses. Licenses with NC (NonCommercial) or ND (NoDerivatives) restrictions are NOT free culture licenses.

### Free Culture Approved CC Licenses

#### CC0 1.0 Universal (Public Domain)

**Full name:** Creative Commons Zero v1.0 Universal
**SPDX:** CC0-1.0
**Status:** Free culture approved

Waives all rights. Closest thing to public domain that works internationally.

**Allows:** Everything, without restriction
**Requires:** Nothing

**Best for:**
- Data and databases
- Works you want in public domain
- Maximum sharing without any conditions

**Text:** https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/

---

#### CC BY 4.0 (Attribution)

**Full name:** Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International
**SPDX:** CC-BY-4.0
**Status:** Free culture approved

**Allows:**
- Commercial use
- Modifications and derivatives
- Any medium or format

**Requires:**
- Attribution (credit, link to license, indicate changes)

**Best for:**
- Academic publications
- Educational content
- Artistic works you want shared widely

**Text:** https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/

---

#### CC BY-SA 4.0 (Attribution-ShareAlike)

**Full name:** Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International
**SPDX:** CC-BY-SA-4.0
**Status:** Free culture approved

**Allows:**
- Commercial use
- Modifications and derivatives

**Requires:**
- Attribution
- ShareAlike (derivatives must use same or compatible license)

**Best for:**
- Wikipedia-style collaborative content
- Open educational resources
- Works where you want derivatives to stay free

**Compatible with:** GPL (one-way: CC BY-SA â†’ GPL allowed, not reverse)

**Text:** https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/

---

### Non-Free CC Licenses (for reference)

These are NOT free culture licenses but are sometimes confused with them:

| License | Why not free |
|---------|--------------|
| CC BY-NC | Commercial restriction |
| CC BY-NC-SA | Commercial restriction |
| CC BY-ND | No derivatives restriction |
| CC BY-NC-ND | Both restrictions |

If a user wants NC or ND restrictions, explain:
- These are valid choices but not "free" or "open"
- May limit adoption and collaboration
- Some communities won't accept NC/ND content

## Free Documentation Licenses

### GNU Free Documentation License (GFDL)

**Full name:** GNU Free Documentation License
**SPDX:** GFDL-1.3-only / GFDL-1.3-or-later
**Type:** Copyleft for documentation

Designed for software documentation, manuals, and textbooks.

**Features:**
- Copyleft (derivatives must be GFDL)
- Allows "Invariant Sections" (cannot be modified)
- Complex compliance requirements

**Note:** The Invariant Sections feature is controversial. Debian considers GFDL with Invariant Sections as non-free.

**Used by:** Wikipedia (historically, now CC BY-SA)

**Text:** https://www.gnu.org/licenses/fdl-1.3.html

---

### FreeBSD Documentation License

Simple permissive license for documentation, similar to BSD 2-Clause.

**Text:** https://www.freebsd.org/copyright/freebsd-doc-license/

---

## Free Licenses for Fonts

### SIL Open Font License 1.1 (OFL)

**Full name:** SIL Open Font License 1.1
**SPDX:** OFL-1.1
**Type:** Copyleft for fonts

**Allows:**
- Use in any document
- Bundling with any software
- Modification and redistribution

**Requires:**
- Derivatives must be OFL
- Derivatives must use different name (Reserved Font Name)
- Cannot be sold standalone

**Best for:**
- Open-source fonts
- Fonts for free distribution
- Web fonts

**Used by:** Google Fonts, most open-source fonts

**Text:** https://scripts.sil.org/OFL

---

### Ubuntu Font License

Similar to OFL, designed for Ubuntu fonts.

**Text:** https://ubuntu.com/legal/font-licence

---

## Free Licenses for Databases

### Open Data Commons Licenses

#### ODC-PDDL (Public Domain Dedication)

Public domain dedication for databases. Similar to CC0.

**Text:** https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/pddl/

---

#### ODC-BY (Attribution)

Attribution license for databases. Similar to CC BY.

**Text:** https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/by/

---

#### ODC-ODbL (Open Database License)

Copyleft license for databases. Similar to CC BY-SA.

**Requires:**
- Attribution
- ShareAlike for derivative databases
- Keep database open

**Used by:** OpenStreetMap

**Text:** https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/odbl/

---

## Free Licenses for Hardware

### CERN Open Hardware Licenses v2

#### CERN-OHL-P (Permissive)

Permissive hardware license, like MIT for hardware.

#### CERN-OHL-W (Weakly Reciprocal)

Weak copyleft, like LGPL for hardware.

#### CERN-OHL-S (Strongly Reciprocal)

Strong copyleft, like GPL for hardware.

**Text:** https://ohwr.org/cernohl

---

### TAPR Open Hardware License

Earlier open hardware license, still used.

**Text:** https://tapr.org/the-tapr-open-hardware-license/

## Quick Reference: Choosing a Free Creative License

### For Maximum Freedom (Public Domain-like)

- **CC0** - Works everywhere, no restrictions
- **ODC-PDDL** - For databases specifically

### For Attribution Only

- **CC BY** - Creative works
- **ODC-BY** - Databases

### For Copyleft/ShareAlike

- **CC BY-SA** - Creative works (Wikipedia-style)
- **ODbL** - Databases (OpenStreetMap-style)
- **OFL** - Fonts
- **Licence Art Libre** - Artistic works (French)

### For Documentation

- **CC BY** or **CC BY-SA** - Generally preferred
- **GFDL** - Legacy, more complex

### For Hardware

- **CERN-OHL-P** - Permissive
- **CERN-OHL-S** - Copyleft

## License Compatibility for Creative Works

| From \ To | CC0 | CC BY | CC BY-SA | GFDL |
|-----------|-----|-------|----------|------|
| CC0 | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| CC BY | No | Yes | Yes | Yes* |
| CC BY-SA | No | No | Yes | No |

*GFDL 1.3 has special Wikipedia compatibility clause with CC BY-SA

---

## What is NOT a Free License

The following restrict freedom and are NOT free culture licenses:

| Restriction | Example | Why not free |
|-------------|---------|--------------|
| Non-commercial | CC BY-NC | Restricts use |
| No derivatives | CC BY-ND | Restricts modification |
| Field of use | Educational only | Restricts use |
| Geographical | Country-specific | Restricts distribution |

If users want these restrictions, they can still use them - but should understand they're choosing something other than a "free" license.
